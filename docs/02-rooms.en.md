# 02 · The Six Rooms

[한국어](02-rooms.md) | **English**

One building, six rooms, three kinds of paths.

- **User space** — Reading Room ↔ Return Desk
- **Observer space** — Reference Librarian's Desk ↔ Director's Office
- **System space** — Cataloguing Room · Stacks (entered by clicking nodes of the diagram, not role buttons — these are the system's places, not people's)

The rooms of roles carry *experience*; the rooms of the system carry *legitimacy* (transparency, accountability).

---

## Reading Room — Worker Input Layer

![Reading Room — checklist and blurred shadows](../assets/reading-room.png)

The worker's screen is **a single cell of blurred glass**.

- Inside one's own capsule. Neighboring workers exist only as *moving shadows* beyond the blur — not lonely, yet separated
- The top is hazily closed, opening upward briefly only at the moment of one's own input
- At the center, a safety checklist (7 items, pass/fail toggles) and a separate *report anomaly* button

**The five intervals of mediation.** From input to response, nothing is processed instantly; five time
intervals intervene — ① the moment of input (1s, retractable) → ② sealed transit (2–3s, retraction is recorded)
→ ③ entry (0.5–1s) → ④ arrival of one's own response (3–4s) → ⑤ arrival of the control order (4–5s).
These times exist not for efficiency but for *experiential weight*.

![Right after reporting an anomaly — mediation ② sealed transit](../assets/reading-room-mediation.png)

**Fluid vs. crystal.** One's own actions and responses behave as soft fluid (condensing, stretching, absorbing);
danger signals and control orders behave as sharp crystal (compressing, crystallizing, lodging).
Danger and the everyday are distinguished not by color but by **states of matter**.

**The branching structure.** Report an anomaly, and cross-validation and your own response circuit engage;
stay silent, and the case is handled as a single-source alert — a passive position that only receives orders.
The experiential proof of the thesis.

## Reference Librarian's Desk — Supervisor Review Layer

![Reference Librarian's Desk — the table and the red pulse of a mismatched row](../assets/reference-desk.png)

The supervisor's console. Inputs from all workers appear as a table sorted by risk.

- **The red pulse of the mismatched row** — the most essential spatial interaction in the system.
  It works without demanding the user's attention: the one element that operates like architecture.
- "Surface is message" — state is conveyed by changes of the background (surface), not text color (site legibility)
- Escalation logic against alarm fatigue (3 min / 5 min / 10 min)
- Hierarchical information density — the table alone by default; structure and evidence expand progressively on row click

## Director's Office — Authority Control Layer

![Director's Office — the vertical spine and the descending decision beam](../assets/directors-office.png)

From the apex of the system, the whole hierarchy is seen down a **vertical spine** —
the L4 console, the L3 supervisor node, L2 cross-validation (including the escalated red case), the L1 worker row.

Three binding decisions: **stop work / conditional continuation / return to supervisor.**
Each decision descends the spine as a directional dither beam, and each reaches differently —
stop and continuation reach every worker; return reaches only the supervisor.
The scope of authority is the reach of light.

## Cataloguing Room — Agent Orchestration Layer

![Cataloguing Room — orchestration graph and step log](../assets/cataloguing.png)

The room of agent orchestration. Inhabited by the system, not by people.

- The orchestration graph: worker input → catalogue → cross-validation → review → escalation to the director
- Packets flow between nodes; a synchronized step-log console records each stage
- A replay button lets the orchestration be observed again — **the room of explainability**

## Stacks — Raw Evidence Archive

![Stacks — rows of records and a lit location](../assets/stacks.png)

The room of pure observation. Nothing can be manipulated.

- Dense rows of records receding toward a horizontal vanishing point
- Click a preserved record on the index console → the record's *location* lights up deep among the shelves (record ↔ place interaction)
- The raw evidence of every judgment is preserved, never erased — **the room of accountability**

## Return Desk — Site Control Output Layer · Not built

The output layer where the system's decisions return to the site. Not built in the current prototype.
It is partially delegated to the way the director's decisions "lodge" at the boundary of the worker's capsule,
but the Return Desk as an independent room remains an open question.
